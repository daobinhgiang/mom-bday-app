# Ngôi nhà của Mẹ — MVP Spec

A web-based illustrated Vietnamese home Mom can walk through. Four rooms, six features. Family contributes secretly; Mom contributes back. Ship the version that makes her cry — then keep building.

---

## Three principles

- **No notifications.** A glowing lantern in a room's window means new content is there.
- **Slowness is a feature.** Doors take a moment to open. The house breathes.
- **She lives here too.** Not a museum about her — she has her own voice from day one.

---

## The reveal (first launch only)

1. Black screen. Rain on tile, distant chime, crickets.
2. Handwriting fades in: *"Mẹ ơi, về nhà thôi."*
3. Camera pulls back: the house at golden hour, lantern glow, cat on porch.
4. She taps the door. It creaks open.
5. Inside, family figures hold paper lanterns. They wave, then walk into different rooms, each leaving an item — Dad sets a record, sister places a book, grandkid tapes a photo to the wall.
6. House goes quiet. The lanterns they left guide her first exploration.

Plays once. Future visits open straight to the house.

---

## House view (the hub)

Cross-section dollhouse — all four rooms visible at once.

- **Day/night cycle.** Morning sun, evening lantern glow, night candles. Tied to real time.
- **A cat** wanders between rooms. Tapping it does nothing — that's the point.
- **Glowing windows** mark rooms with new content.
- **Tap a room** to enter. Tap the front-door icon inside any room to return.

Cut from MVP: weather sync, holiday dressing, logbook index.

---

## Rooms

### 1. Library — Thư viện

A wall of bookshelves, an armchair, her writing desk in the corner.

**Features:**

- **Family books.** Each family member has one book on the shelf. Spine reflects them — thick leather for Aunt's letters, crayon paperback for a grandkid. Tap → it slides off, opens with a page-turn sound. Read chapters with embedded photos.
- **Mom's journal.** A blank book on her writing desk. She types or dictates entries in Vietnamese, attaches photos. Private by default; she can toggle an entry to "share to library" so it appears as her own book on the shelf.

Cut from MVP: audio narration of chapters, ribbon bookmarks, guestbook.

### 2. Music room — Phòng nhạc

Turntable on a shelf, wooden crate of records, brass floor lamp.

**Features:**

- **Records with dedications.** Each family member contributes one song with hand-illustrated cover art. Tap a record → it lifts onto the turntable, needle drops, song plays. A card flips up next to the player: who picked it and why ("This was the song you used to hum while doing my hair before school"). Note can be text or voice.

Cut from MVP: piano with karaoke, Mom's microphone, music-reactive room visuals.

### 3. Map room — Phòng bản đồ

Floor-to-ceiling map, drawer of postcards, old leather suitcase.

**Features:**

- **Pinned places with postcards.** Family pins meaningful places. Tap a pin → a postcard slides out and flips onto the desk: photos, short story, optional voice memo. Mom can drop her own pins for places only she remembers.

Cut from MVP: pin color system (green/orange/gold), dream-pin-becomes-real mechanic, plane tickets.

### 4. Family room — Phòng gia đình

Photo frames covering the walls. A shelf with a glass jar.

**Features:**

- **Photo wall.** Family tapes up photos. New ones tilted, older ones settled. Tap any photo to enlarge with caption and uploader name.
- **Memory jar.** Folded notes — things family loves about her. She pulls one per day. The note unfolds and reads itself in the writer's handwriting style. The jar never empties.

Cut from MVP: TV with home videos, vintage answering machine, fridge with grandkid drawings, photo album, "today's photo" frame.

---

## How content surfaces

The house signals organically. No badges, no red dots.

- Lantern glows in a window with new content.
- Inside the room, the new item is the most visually prominent (a new book sticks slightly off the shelf, a new record sticks out of the crate, a new pin pulses, a new photo is tilted slightly more).
- Cat sometimes sits next to what's newest.

---

## How family contributes

Each family member has their own login. They see a simple contribution view per content type.

- **Add a book chapter** → upload photos, write text, save.
- **Add a record** → upload song or paste streaming link, upload cover art, write/record dedication.
- **Add a pin** → search location, upload photos, write story, optional voice memo.
- **Add a photo** to the family room wall.
- **Add a note** to the memory jar.

Cut from MVP: drag-and-drop placement, signature lantern colors per person, scheduled appearance dates, co-curation.

---

## How Mom contributes

She has two contribution surfaces from day one:

- **Library** — her journal (private or share-to-library)
- **Map room** — her own pins

Cut from MVP: her recordings, her photos, her answering-machine messages, future letters, lockbox.

---

## Closing the app

Lights dim across the house room by room. *"Hẹn gặp Mẹ ngày mai."* The cat curls up. Black.

---

## UX principles

- **Slowness is a feature.** Animations are unhurried.
- **No menus.** Volume is a lantern dial. Logout is closing the front door.
- **One action per tap.** Always a clear way back.
- **Mobile + desktop.** Cross-section view on both. Rooms full-screen on both.

---

## Content checklist for launch

- Reveal sequence (animated, with audio)
- 4 rooms illustrated (cross-section + interior views)
- 1 record per family member with dedication
- 1 chapter per family member in their book
- 5–10 pinned places with postcards
- 20–30 photos on the family room wall
- 10–15 notes in the memory jar

---

## What's deferred to v1.1+

- Garden (clothesline, vegetable patch, swing, koi pond)
- Bedroom (future letters, lockbox, wardrobe of lanterns)
- Music room: karaoke, her microphone
- Map room: dream pins, plane-ticket transformations
- Family room: TV, answering machine, fridge, photo album
- Library: audio narration, guestbook
- House: weather sync, holiday dressing, logbook
- Family: scheduled contributions, signature lantern colors, co-curation
- Mom: her own recordings and photos across rooms

---

## The one thing to get right

**The illustration style.** Tile roof, wooden shutters, *bàn thờ* niche, woven mats, ceramic tea set, the specific yellow of old Saigon walls. Commission a Vietnamese illustrator if you can — AI-generated art will feel slightly off and Mom will sense it without knowing why.

References: Studio Ghibli interiors, Tô Ngọc Vân's warm domestic palettes, Stephen Biesty cross-sections, *Animal Crossing* interiors.

Everything else can be rebuilt. The illustration is the soul.
